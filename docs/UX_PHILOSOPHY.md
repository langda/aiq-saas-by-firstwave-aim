# AIQ — UX Philosophy (Founder Decision 20, 2026-07-16)

> Source of truth for every UX decision from this point forward. AIQ is an
> **identity platform**, not an assessment platform. Users leave saying
> "I discovered my AI work style" — never "I completed an assessment."
> **People won't remember every question we asked. They will remember how
> AIQ made them feel.**

## The Four Rules

1. **The Smile Rule (highest priority).** The user should smile at least
   three times during the assessment — from curiosity, delight,
   encouragement, anticipation, surprise, or pride. Not jokes. Every screen,
   animation, transition, and line of microcopy must pass: _"Will this
   moment make the user smile?"_
2. **The Screenshot Test.** Every major milestone (persona reveal,
   achievement screen, certificate, progress milestones) must be beautiful
   enough that users naturally screenshot and share it.
3. **The 10-Second Rule.** Within 10 seconds of seeing their result, users
   understand who they are, why it matters, and why they should feel proud.
   Emotion first; analysis later, below the fold.
4. **The No Shame Rule.** No bad personas, no failing scores, no
   disappointing outcomes. Growth opportunities inspire curiosity, never
   expose weakness. We celebrate potential, not perfection. Banned level
   words: Beginner, Novice, Weak, Poor, Low.

## Product Rules

- **Achievements over analytics**: no raw "87/100" on primary surfaces —
  visual achievement levels (stars + aspirational level names) instead.
  Numbers live in expandable detail (and on the certificate per Decision 6).
- **Sharing is a first-class feature**: share cards must be minimal,
  premium, instantly recognizable, understandable in 3 seconds, and carry
  identity — never analytics.
- **Reduce reading**: challenge every paragraph. Prefer visuals, icons,
  badges, short phrases. If a sentence doesn't add excitement or usefulness,
  delete it.
- **Feeling, not imitation**: capture the emotional quality of Duolingo /
  Spotify Wrapped / Apple Fitness / game achievements — never copy their
  design.
- Small emotional moments beat complicated gamification.

## Hard Constraints (unchanged)

Architecture, assessment engine, scoring engine, and database design are
frozen. This philosophy governs the **presentation layer**: UI, motion,
microcopy, share surfaces, and display-level configuration (e.g.
achievement-level mapping lives in config, not in the scoring engine).

## The Test of Success

If someone finishes AIQ and doesn't immediately want to show at least one
friend, the product experience has failed.
